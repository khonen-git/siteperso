# Fundamentals

<MdxCard>
This page covers core probability concepts: axiomatic framework, random variables, moments, and limit theorems. For concrete laws, see [Distributions](/knowledge/probability/distributions).
</MdxCard>

## Axiomatic framework

<MdxCard>
A probability <MathInline>P</MathInline> on a space <MathInline>{"\\Omega"}</MathInline> satisfies:

1. <MathInline>{"P(A) \\geq 0"}</MathInline> for every event <MathInline>A</MathInline>
2. <MathInline>{"P(\\Omega) = 1"}</MathInline>
3. <MathInline>{"\\sigma"}</MathInline>-additivity: for disjoint events <MathInline>{"A_i"}</MathInline>,

<MathBlock>{"P\\left(\\bigcup_{i} A_i\\right) = \\sum_{i} P(A_i)"}</MathBlock>
</MdxCard>

## Probability space

<MdxCard>
A **probability space** is a triple <MathInline>{"(\\Omega, \\mathcal{F}, P)"}</MathInline>:

- <MathInline>{"\\Omega"}</MathInline>: sample space
- <MathInline>{"\\mathcal{F}"}</MathInline>: sigma-algebra of measurable events
- <MathInline>P</MathInline>: probability measure

The sigma-algebra supports countable limits on events.
</MdxCard>

## Random variable and distribution

<MdxCard>
A **random variable** <MathInline>X</MathInline> is a measurable map <MathInline>{"X : \\Omega \\to \\mathbb{R}"}</MathInline> (or <MathInline>{"\\mathbb{R}^d"}</MathInline>).

Its **law** is the pushforward measure <MathInline>{"P_X(B) = P(X \\in B)"}</MathInline>.

- **Discrete**: mass <MathInline>{"p_X(x) = P(X = x)"}</MathInline>
- **Continuous**: density <MathInline>{"f_X"}</MathInline> with <MathInline>{"P(X \\in B) = \\int_B f_X(x)\\,dx"}</MathInline>

CDF: <MathInline>{"F_X(x) = P(X \\leq x)"}</MathInline>.
</MdxCard>

## Expectation

<MdxCard>
**Expectation** is the center of mass of a law:

<MathBlock>{"\\mathbb{E}[X] = \\sum_x x\\,p_X(x) \\quad \\text{or} \\quad \\int_{\\mathbb{R}} x f_X(x)\\,dx"}</MathBlock>

**Linearity**: <MathInline>{"\\mathbb{E}[aX + bY] = a\\mathbb{E}[X] + b\\mathbb{E}[Y]"}</MathInline> (no independence required).

**Transfer**: <MathInline>{"\\mathbb{E}[g(X)] = \\int g(x)\\,dP_X(x)"}</MathInline>.
</MdxCard>

## Variance, covariance, and correlation

<MdxCard>
<MathBlock>{"\\mathrm{Var}(X) = \\mathbb{E}[(X - \\mathbb{E}[X])^2] = \\mathbb{E}[X^2] - (\\mathbb{E}[X])^2"}</MathBlock>

<MathBlock>{"\\mathrm{Cov}(X,Y) = \\mathbb{E}[(X-\\mathbb{E}[X])(Y-\\mathbb{E}[Y])]"}</MathBlock>

<MathBlock>{"\\rho_{X,Y} = \\frac{\\mathrm{Cov}(X,Y)}{\\sigma_X \\sigma_Y} \\in [-1, 1]"}</MathBlock>

For a sum: <MathInline>{"\\mathrm{Var}(X+Y) = \\mathrm{Var}(X) + \\mathrm{Var}(Y) + 2\\mathrm{Cov}(X,Y)"}</MathInline>; if <MathInline>X</MathInline> and <MathInline>Y</MathInline> are independent, the cross term vanishes.
</MdxCard>

## Moments, skewness, and kurtosis

<MdxCard>
The <MathInline>k</MathInline>-th moment: <MathInline>{"\\mathbb{E}[X^k]"}</MathInline>. Central moments of order 3 and 4 define:

- **Skewness**: asymmetry of the distribution
- **Kurtosis**: tail heaviness relative to the normal

In finance, high kurtosis signals **tail risk**.
</MdxCard>

## Conditional law and independence

<MdxCard>
Conditional law of <MathInline>X</MathInline> given <MathInline>Y</MathInline>:

<MathBlock>{"P(X \\in A \\mid Y = y) = \\frac{P(X \\in A, Y = y)}{P(Y = y)}"}</MathBlock>

**Independence**: <MathInline>{"P(X \\in A, Y \\in B) = P(X \\in A)\\,P(Y \\in B)"}</MathInline>, or <MathInline>{"f_{X,Y}(x,y) = f_X(x) f_Y(y)"}</MathInline>.

Independence <MathInline>{"\\neq"}</MathInline> uncorrelatedness (except in the Gaussian case).
</MdxCard>

## Law of large numbers

<MdxCard>
For i.i.d. <MathInline>{"X_1, \\ldots, X_n"}</MathInline> with <MathInline>{"\\mathbb{E}[|X_1|] < \\infty"}</MathInline>:

<MathBlock>{"\\bar{X}_n = \\frac{1}{n}\\sum_{i=1}^n X_i \\xrightarrow[n\\to\\infty]{} \\mathbb{E}[X_1]"}</MathBlock>

The sample mean converges to the true mean — foundation of Monte Carlo and long backtests.
</MdxCard>

## Central limit theorem

<MdxCard>
If <MathInline>{"\\mathbb{E}[X_1]=\\mu"}</MathInline> and <MathInline>{"\\mathrm{Var}(X_1)=\\sigma^2 < \\infty"}</MathInline>:

<MathBlock>{"\\frac{\\bar{X}_n - \\mu}{\\sigma / \\sqrt{n}} \\xrightarrow[n\\to\\infty]{\\mathcal{L}} \\mathcal{N}(0,1)"}</MathBlock>

Justifies widespread use of the [normal distribution](/knowledge/probability/distributions/normal) and approximation of estimation errors.
</MdxCard>
