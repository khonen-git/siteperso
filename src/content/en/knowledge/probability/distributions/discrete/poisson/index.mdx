# Poisson distribution

<MdxCard title="Definition">
The **Poisson distribution** models the **number of events** over an interval (time, area, volume) when events are **rare**, **independent**, and occur at a **constant rate** <MathInline>{"\\lambda"}</MathInline>. It arises as the limit of the [binomial](/knowledge/probability/distributions/discrete/binomial) and as the jump law of a Poisson process.
</MdxCard>

      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell className="font-medium">Probability mass function</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"P(X = k) = e^{-\\lambda} \\frac{\\lambda^k}{k!}"}</MathInline></TableCell>

      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell className="font-medium">Cumulative distribution function</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"F(k) = P(X \\leq k) = e^{-\\lambda} \\sum_{j=0}^{k} \\frac{\\lambda^j}{j!}"}</MathInline></TableCell>

      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell className="font-medium">Moment-generating function</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"M_X(t) = \\exp\\bigl(\\lambda(e^t - 1)\\bigr)"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Derivatives at 0 yield moments</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell className="font-medium">Characteristic function</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"\\varphi_X(t) = \\exp\\bigl(\\lambda(e^{it} - 1)\\bigr)"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Fourier transform of the law</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell className="font-medium">Mean</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"\\mathbb{E}[X] = \\lambda"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Equals the intensity parameter</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell className="font-medium">Variance</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"\\mathrm{Var}(X) = \\lambda"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell><strong>Equidispersion</strong>: mean = variance (diagnostic property)</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell className="font-medium">Standard deviation</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"\\sigma_X = \\sqrt{\\lambda}"}</MathInline></TableCell>

      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell className="font-medium">Mode</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"\\lfloor \\lambda \\rfloor"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Two modes <MathInline>{"\\lambda"}</MathInline> and <MathInline>{"\\lambda - 1"}</MathInline> if <MathInline>{"\\lambda"}</MathInline> is a positive integer</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell className="font-medium">Median</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"\\lfloor \\lambda \\rfloor"}</MathInline> or <MathInline>{"\\lceil \\lambda \\rceil"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Common approximation: <MathInline>{"\\lambda - \\frac{1}{3}"}</MathInline> for large <MathInline>{"\\lambda"}</MathInline></TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell className="font-medium">Skewness</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"\\gamma_1 = \\frac{1}{\\sqrt{\\lambda}}"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Right-skewed; tends to 0 as <MathInline>{"\\lambda \\to \\infty"}</MathInline></TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell className="font-medium">Excess kurtosis</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"\\gamma_2 = \\frac{1}{\\lambda}"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Heavier tails than the normal for small <MathInline>{"\\lambda"}</MathInline></TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell className="font-medium">Shannon entropy</TableCell>
        <TableCell>—</TableCell>
        <TableCell>No simple closed form; numerical computation via the PMF</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell className="font-medium">Fisher information</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"\\mathcal{I}(\\lambda) = \\frac{1}{\\lambda}"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>For one observation; decreases with intensity</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell className="font-medium">Kullback-Leibler divergence</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"D_{\\mathrm{KL}}(\\lambda_1 \\| \\lambda_2) = \\lambda_1 \\ln\\frac{\\lambda_1}{\\lambda_2} + \\lambda_2 - \\lambda_1"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Between two Poisson laws with rates <MathInline>{"\\lambda_1"}</MathInline> and <MathInline>{"\\lambda_2"}</MathInline></TableCell>
      </TableRow>
    </TableBody>

        <TableHead>Description</TableHead>
      </TableRow>
    </TableHeader>
    <TableBody>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>Binomial limit</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"n \\to \\infty,\\; p = \\lambda/n \\Rightarrow \\mathrm{Bin}(n,p) \\to \\mathrm{Poisson}(\\lambda)"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Link to the [binomial](/knowledge/probability/distributions/discrete/binomial) in the rare regime</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>Additivity</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"X \\sim \\mathrm{Poisson}(\\lambda_1),\\; Y \\sim \\mathrm{Poisson}(\\lambda_2)"}</MathInline> indep. <MathInline>{"\\Rightarrow X+Y \\sim \\mathrm{Poisson}(\\lambda_1+\\lambda_2)"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Intensities add over disjoint intervals</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>Thinning</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"X \\sim \\mathrm{Poisson}(\\lambda)"}</MathInline>, each event kept with prob. <MathInline>p</MathInline> <MathInline>{"\\Rightarrow \\mathrm{Poisson}(\\lambda p)"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Independent filtering of events; basis for jump models</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>Equidispersion</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"\\mathbb{E}[X] = \\mathrm{Var}(X) = \\lambda"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>If <MathInline>{"\\mathrm{Var} \\gg \\mathbb{E}"}</MathInline> empirically: **overdispersion** (negative binomial, etc.)</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>Normal approximation</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"\\frac{X - \\lambda}{\\sqrt{\\lambda}} \\xrightarrow{\\mathcal{L}} \\mathcal{N}(0,1)"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>For large <MathInline>{"\\lambda"}</MathInline>; see [normal distribution](/knowledge/probability/distributions/normal)</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>Link to the exponential</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"N_t \\sim \\mathrm{Poisson}(\\lambda t)"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Number of arrivals by time <MathInline>t</MathInline> in a Poisson process of rate <MathInline>{"\\lambda"}</MathInline></TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>Random sum</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"N \\sim \\mathrm{Poisson}(\\mu),\\; X_i \\text{ i.i.d.} \\Rightarrow \\sum_{i=1}^N X_i"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Compound Poisson; aggregate loss models in insurance</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>Independent disjoint counts</TableCell>
        <TableCell>—</TableCell>
        <TableCell>Event counts on disjoint intervals are independent Poisson variables</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>Chernoff bound</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"P(X \\geq (1+\\delta)\\lambda) \\leq e^{-\\delta^2 \\lambda / 3}"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Exponential upper tail bound</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
    </TableBody>

## Applications

<div className="grid gap-4 md:grid-cols-2">

<MdxCard>

### Arrivals and flows

Number of orders, trades, or network packets per minute/hour under constant rate and independence assumptions.

</MdxCard>

<MdxCard>

### Defaults and claims

Number of defaults in a very granular portfolio or claims in non-life insurance (baseline frequency model).

</MdxCard>

<MdxCard>

### Physics and reliability

Radioactive decays, rare equipment failures, production anomalies on a line.

</MdxCard>

<MdxCard>

### Queues and microstructure

Elementary modeling of liquidity arrivals or ticks; extension to compound Poisson for price jumps.

</MdxCard>

## Statistical inference

<MdxCard>
  <Table>
    <TableHeader>
      <TableRow>
        <TableHead>Concept</TableHead>
        <TableHead>Formula</TableHead>
        <TableHead>Description</TableHead>
      </TableRow>
    </TableHeader>
    <TableBody>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>Sample</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"X_1, \\ldots, X_n \\stackrel{\\mathrm{i.i.d.}}{\\sim} \\mathrm{Poisson}(\\lambda)"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>n</MathInline> independent intervals or observations</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>Sufficient statistic</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"S = \\sum_{i=1}^n X_i \\sim \\mathrm{Poisson}(n\\lambda)"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>S</MathInline> summarizes all information on <MathInline>{"\\lambda"}</MathInline></TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>MLE estimator</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"\\hat{\\lambda} = \\bar{X}_n = \\frac{S}{n}"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Sample mean; unbiased, consistent, efficient</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>Estimator variance</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"\\mathrm{Var}(\\hat{\\lambda}) = \\frac{\\lambda}{n}"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Decays as <MathInline>{"1/n"}</MathInline></TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>Exact interval (<MathInline>{"\\chi^2"}</MathInline>)</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"\\left[\\frac{\\chi^2_{2S,\\,\\alpha/2}}{2n},\\; \\frac{\\chi^2_{2(S+1),\\,1-\\alpha/2}}{2n}\\right]"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Exact CI for <MathInline>{"\\lambda"}</MathInline> from sum <MathInline>S</MathInline></TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>Wald interval</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"\\hat{\\lambda} \\pm z_{1-\\alpha/2} \\sqrt{\\frac{\\hat{\\lambda}}{n}}"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Normal approximation; acceptable when <MathInline>{"n\\hat{\\lambda}"}</MathInline> is large</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>Exact test</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"P(S = s \\mid \\lambda = \\lambda_0) = \\frac{(n\\lambda_0)^s e^{-n\\lambda_0}}{s!}"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Exact p-value under <MathInline>{"H_0 : \\lambda = \\lambda_0"}</MathInline></TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>Asymptotic test</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"Z = \\frac{\\hat{\\lambda} - \\lambda_0}{\\sqrt{\\lambda_0/n}} \\approx \\mathcal{N}(0,1)"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>Simple alternative for large <MathInline>{"n\\lambda_0"}</MathInline></TableCell>
      </TableRow>
      <TableRow>
        <TableCell>Conjugate prior (Bayesian)</TableCell>
        <TableCell><MathInline>{"\\lambda \\sim \\mathrm{Gamma}(\\alpha, \\beta) \\Rightarrow \\lambda \\mid \\mathbf{x} \\sim \\mathrm{Gamma}(\\alpha + S, \\beta + n)"}</MathInline></TableCell>
        <TableCell>The gamma law is conjugate to Poisson</TableCell>
      </TableRow>
    </TableBody>
  </Table>
</MdxCard>

<MdxCard>
  <div className="space-y-4">
    <h3 className="text-xl font-medium">Important remarks</h3>
    <ul className="list-disc pl-6 space-y-2">
      <li><strong>Equidispersion</strong> (<MathInline>{"\\bar{X} \\approx s^2"}</MathInline>) is a first diagnostic: a large gap suggests an alternative model.</li>
      <li>Overdispersion is common in finance and insurance, motivating negative binomial or random-effects models.</li>
      <li>The <MathInline>{"\\chi^2"}</MathInline>-based interval is preferable to Wald for small <MathInline>{"\\lambda"}</MathInline> or moderate <MathInline>n</MathInline>.</li>
      <li>Comparing two rates <MathInline>{"\\lambda_1"}</MathInline> and <MathInline>{"\\lambda_2"}</MathInline> across samples amounts to testing equality of two Poisson means.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</MdxCard>

  <CodeBlock language="python">

import numpy as np
from scipy import stats

lam = 4.5
n_periods = 52  # e.g. weeks
counts = np.array([3, 6, 5, 4, 7, 2, 5, 4, 6, 3])  # partial observations
n = len(counts)

# Theoretical law
pois = stats.poisson(lam)
print(f"E[X] = {pois.mean():.2f}, Var(X) = {pois.var():.2f}")
print(f"P(X = 5) = {pois.pmf(5):.4f}")

# MLE
lam_hat = counts.mean()
S = counts.sum()
print(f"λ̂ = {lam_hat:.3f}")

# Exact 95% CI via chi²
alpha = 0.05
ci_low = stats.chi2.ppf(alpha / 2, 2 * S) / (2 * n)
ci_high = stats.chi2.ppf(1 - alpha / 2, 2 * (S + 1)) / (2 * n)
print(f"Exact 95% CI: [{ci_low:.3f}, {ci_high:.3f}]")

# Test H0: λ = 4.0
result = stats.poisson.cdf(S, mu=4.0 * n)  # one-sided approach on the sum
print(f"S = {S}, E[S|λ=4] = {4.0 * n}")

# Simulation
rng = np.random.default_rng(42)
samples = rng.poisson(lam, size=10_000)
print(f"Simulated mean: {samples.mean():.3f}")
`}
