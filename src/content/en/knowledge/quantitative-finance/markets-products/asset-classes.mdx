# Asset classes

<MdxCard>
  In market finance, an **asset class** groups instruments that share similar return and risk drivers (rates, growth, USD, volatility, etc.).  
  This is not the same as a **contract type** (spot, future, option…): one class can be traded through many vehicles — see [Contract types](/knowledge/quantitative-finance/markets-products/contract-types).
</MdxCard>

## Correlations (quick reminder)

<MdxCard>
  <div className="space-y-2">
    <p>Cross-class correlations are not stable: they often rise in stress (flight to quality, simultaneous risk-asset selling).</p>
    <ul className="list-disc pl-6 space-y-1">
      <li><strong>Risk-on</strong>: equities, crypto, carry FX often move together.</li>
      <li><strong>Risk-off</strong>: sovereign bonds, USD, gold (often); spreads compress until credit breaks.</li>
      <li><strong>Vol</strong>: tends to rise when equities fall (except short-lived decorrelation).</li>
    </ul>
    <p className="text-muted-foreground text-sm">To add: regime correlation matrices, historical examples (2008, 2020, 2022…).</p>
  </div>
</MdxCard>
